Privacy Policy for Bhandari Pariwar

Last updated: April 7, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Bhandari Pariwar mobile application ("Bhandari Pariwar," the "App," "we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when people use the App. It reflects the App's current features, including account signup, admin approval, family tree access, notices, language selection, notification permissions, and administrator editing tools.

Privacy contact: thesigmoit@gmail.com

1. Scope

The App is used to preserve and present family heritage information, family tree records, member profiles, notices, committee information, memorial and elder sayings, acknowledgements, and related photo galleries. This Privacy Policy applies to information processed through the App and its connected backend services.

2. Information We Collect

  • Signup and account request information: full name, contact number, email address, address, account status, role, signup timestamps, approval timestamps, approver information, last sign-in timestamps, and related account metadata.
  • Password and sign-in credentials: passwords are processed by Firebase Authentication. We do not intentionally store plain-text passwords in our own application database.
  • Family profile and heritage data: names, gender, family relationships, birth and death dates, birthplace, current and permanent address, father and mother names, phone numbers, email addresses, education or profession, blood group, family counts, notes, and profile photos that are entered or managed through the App.
  • Committee and heritage content: committee member names, positions, phone numbers, photos, family overview content, history content, memorial sayings, elder sayings, acknowledgements, and similar informational content displayed in the App.
  • Notices and notice media: notice titles, notice body text, publication date, optional images, notification settings, and the identifier of the administrator account that created or updated the notice.
  • Photos selected for upload: when an administrator selects a photo from a device, the App may access the chosen image, optionally crop or compress it, and upload it to cloud storage for display inside the App.
  • Notification and device-related data: push notification token, platform type, notification permission status, and related identifiers needed to operate Firebase Cloud Messaging and other backend services.
  • Local settings and session data: selected language, notification preference, whether onboarding prompts were completed, and sign-in session information that helps keep a user logged in until logout or session expiry.

3. How We Use Information

  • To create and manage signup requests and approved user accounts.
  • To let administrators review and approve account access.
  • To allow approved users to sign in and view family tree, notices, galleries, and heritage content.
  • To allow administrators to add, update, and remove family records and App content.
  • To upload, store, and display images used in profiles, notices, committee sections, sayings, and gallery pages.
  • To remember language and notification preferences on the device and sync related account settings where needed.
  • To send important notices and account-related notifications when notification permission is enabled.
  • To maintain, secure, monitor, and troubleshoot the App and its backend services.

4. How Information Is Shared

  • With approved App users: family records, notices, committee details, sayings, gallery images, and related heritage content entered into the App may be viewable to signed-in users whose access has been approved.
  • With administrators: account request information may be visible to administrators so they can review, approve, or manage access requests and maintain App content.
  • With service providers: we use Google Firebase (including Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Cloud Messaging, and related services) and Supabase (including cloud storage and related access/session services) to operate the App.
  • With cloud-hosted image delivery: uploaded images may be stored and served through cloud storage URLs so they can be displayed in the App.
  • For legal or security reasons: we may disclose information if required by law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, or the security of the App and its users.

We do not sell personal data.

5. Third-Party Services

The App currently relies on third-party infrastructure providers, primarily Google Firebase and Supabase, to authenticate users, store structured content, store and deliver images, and support notification delivery. Those providers may process device, network, and service metadata as necessary to provide their services.

Based on the App configuration reviewed on April 7, 2026, the App is not configured to use third-party advertising SDKs and is not configured with a dedicated analytics SDK.

6. Notifications and Permissions

During onboarding, the App may ask users to choose a language and grant notification permission. If notification permission is granted, the App may store a device notification token and related platform information so it can deliver important notices and account-related updates. Users can later change notification settings in the App or through device settings.

7. Data Retention

  • Signup request and account profile records are retained while the account request is pending or the account remains active, unless deleted earlier through the App or administrative action.
  • If a user deletes a pending signup request through the App, the related request record is deleted from the application database, and the related authentication account may also be removed from Firebase Authentication where supported by the sign-in state and security flow.
  • Family records, notices, committee content, sayings, and uploaded images are retained until they are updated or deleted through the App or are no longer needed for App operation.
  • Push notification tokens are retained until notifications are disabled, the token is refreshed or invalidated, the App is uninstalled, or the token record is deleted from backend systems.
  • Local device settings remain on the device until the user changes them, clears App data, or uninstalls the App.
  • Backup copies or technical logs may remain for a limited time where reasonably necessary for security, legal, or operational purposes.

8. Security

We use commercially reasonable measures intended to protect information, including established cloud service providers, authentication controls, role-based permissions, and managed backend infrastructure. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Data About Children and Family Members

The App may include information about family members of different ages, including minors, when that information is provided for legitimate family record, heritage, or communication purposes. If you are a parent, guardian, or family representative and want information corrected or removed, contact us using the privacy contact listed above.

10. Your Choices and Rights

  • You can disable notifications from the App settings or your device settings.
  • You can change your selected language inside the App settings.
  • You can request correction, update, or deletion of personal information by contacting us.
  • You can log out from the App settings.
  • If your signup request is still pending, you may delete that request through the App.
  • Administrators can update or delete certain records directly through the App's management features.

11. International Processing

Because the App uses cloud providers such as Google Firebase and Supabase, information may be stored or processed on servers located in countries other than the country in which the user resides.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the App, legal requirements, or our data practices. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes may also be communicated through the App or another appropriate method.
